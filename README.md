#  Bank Marketing Campaign Analysis | Excel + SQL

## Project Overview

Marketing campaigns are expensive, and contacting the wrong customers wastes both time and resources. Financial institutions need to understand **who is most likely to subscribe to a term deposit** and **what factors influence customer decisions** so they can improve future marketing campaigns.

In this project, I analyzed a real-world bank marketing dataset to uncover customer behavior, identify high-converting customer segments, and provide data-driven recommendations that can improve future campaign effectiveness. 

This project demonstrates my ability to combine **business understanding, SQL, Excel analytics, dashboard development, and data storytelling** to solve a practical business problem.

---

# Business Problem

A financial institution conducted a direct marketing campaign to encourage customers to subscribe to a term deposit.

Management wanted answers to the following questions:

* Why do some customers subscribe while others do not?
* Which customer groups respond best to marketing campaigns?
* Which customer segments should receive more attention?
* Which business factors influence campaign success?
* What risks and opportunities exist?
* How can future marketing campaigns become more effective?

The goal of this project was not simply to visualize data, but to generate actionable business insights that decision makers can use.

# Key Insights

### 1. Older Customers Responded Better

Customers aged **65 years and above** recorded the highest subscription rate, suggesting this segment is more receptive to investment-related products.

### 2. Previous Campaign Success Strongly Influences Future Success

Customers who previously responded positively to a campaign were significantly more likely to subscribe again.

This indicates that customer history should play a larger role in future targeting strategies.

### 3. Repeated Contact Reduces Campaign Effectiveness

Customers contacted multiple times showed declining conversion rates.

This suggests diminishing returns from repeated follow-up calls.

### 4. Housing Loans Affect Customer Decisions

Customers without housing loans demonstrated higher subscription rates, indicating existing financial commitments may influence investment decisions.

### 5. Customer Occupation Matters

Certain occupations showed stronger engagement with marketing campaigns, highlighting the importance of customer segmentation.

### 6. Customer Balance Influences Subscription

Customers with higher account balances generally showed greater interest in term deposits.

---

# Business Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following recommendations were made:

### 1. Prioritize High-Converting Segments

Focus future marketing campaigns on customer groups with historically higher subscription rates.

### 2. Reduce Excessive Follow-Up Calls

Limit repeated campaign contacts and redirect marketing efforts toward new prospects with higher conversion potential.

### 3. Use Previous Campaign Performance

Leverage previous campaign outcomes when selecting customers for future marketing initiatives.

### 4. Personalize Customer Communication

Develop customized marketing messages based on customer demographics, financial profiles, and previous interactions.

### 5. Improve Marketing Efficiency

Allocate marketing budgets toward customer segments with higher conversion probabilities to maximize campaign ROI.

---

# Risks

- Over calling fatigue erodes engagement past 3 attempts.
- Over reliance on small high converting segments limits scale.
- Off-peak month campaigns (e.g. May) risk wasted budget.
  
---

# Opportunities

- Prior-contact history is a hugely underused targeting lever.
- Fixing "unknown-method" contacts to cellular could lift response.
- Shifting timing toward September–December and March costs nothing extra
